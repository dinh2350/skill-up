# HR English Interview Questions for Developers (Vietnam)

## Purpose
This document provides HR-focused interview questions to assess English communication skills of developers during recruitment, covering salary negotiation, personal qualities, career goals, and workplace situations.

---

## 1. OPENING & INTRODUCTION

### Basic Questions (A2-B1)
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why are you looking for a new job?
3. How did you hear about this position?
4. What do you know about our company?
5. Why do you want to work for our company?

### Follow-up Questions
- What interests you most about this role?
- What makes you a good fit for this position?
- Have you applied to other companies?

## 2. SALARY & COMPENSATION

### Initial Salary Discussion (B1-B2)
1. What is your current salary?
2. What are your salary expectations?
3. What is your expected salary range?
4. Are you negotiable on salary?
5. Besides salary, what benefits are important to you?

### Advanced Salary Negotiation (B2-C1)
6. How did you arrive at this salary expectation?
7. What would you do if we cannot meet your salary requirement?
8. Can you explain the gap between your current salary and expected salary?
9. Are you willing to start at a lower salary with a review after probation?
10. What is more important to you: base salary or total compensation package?

### Common Scenarios to Practice

**Scenario 1: Current salary is low**
```
HR: "Your expected salary is much higher than your current salary. Why?"
Good Answer: "My current salary doesn't reflect my market value. Based on my 
research of similar positions and my skill level, I believe [X amount] is fair 
for this role. I'm also taking on more responsibilities in this new position."
```

**Scenario 2: Asked about minimum acceptable salary**
```
HR: "What is the minimum salary you would accept?"
Good Answer: "I'm looking for a fair salary that reflects my experience and 
the value I can bring. Based on my research, the market range for this position 
is [X-Y]. I'm flexible within this range depending on the overall package and 
growth opportunities."
```

**Scenario 3: Company offer is below expectation**
```
HR: "Our budget for this role is [lower amount]. What do you think?"
Good Answer: "I appreciate the offer. However, based on my experience and the 
responsibilities of this role, I was expecting something closer to [amount]. 
Is there flexibility in the budget, or are there other benefits we can discuss 
to bridge this gap?"
```

## 3. AVAILABILITY & NOTICE PERIOD

### Basic Questions (A2-B1)
1. When can you start?
2. When are you available to begin work?
3. How much notice do you need to give your current employer?
4. Are you currently employed?
5. How soon can you join us?

### Detailed Questions (B1-B2)
6. What is your notice period at your current company?
7. Can you start earlier if needed?
8. Do you have any commitments that might delay your start date?
9. Are you interviewing with other companies? When do you need to decide?
10. If we make an offer today, when is the earliest you could start?

### Sample Responses

**If currently employed:**
"I'm currently employed and need to give [X weeks] notice to my company. 
I can start [specific date]. However, I'm willing to discuss the handover 
process with my current employer to see if we can shorten this period if needed."

**If available immediately:**
"I'm currently available and can start immediately, or at any time that's 
convenient for the team. I just need a few days to prepare."

**If need time:**
"I would need approximately [time period] to wrap up my current responsibilities 
and ensure a smooth handover. I believe [specific date] would be realistic."

## 4. STRENGTHS & WEAKNESSES

### Strengths Questions (B1-B2)
1. What are your strengths?
2. What is your biggest strength?
3. What makes you unique as a developer?
4. What can you bring to our team?
5. What are you best at?
6. What would your colleagues say are your strengths?

### Sample Strength Answers for Developers

**Example 1: Problem-solving**
"My biggest strength is problem-solving. I enjoy analyzing complex issues and 
finding efficient solutions. For example, in my last project, I optimized a 
slow query that reduced response time by 60%."

**Example 2: Fast learner**
"I'm a fast learner. When my team needed to migrate to a new technology, I 
learned it quickly and even helped train other team members. I believe this 
adaptability will help me contribute to your team quickly."

**Example 3: Team collaboration**
"I work well in teams. I communicate clearly, share knowledge, and support my 
colleagues. I believe good teamwork is essential for successful projects."

**Example 4: Attention to detail**
"I pay attention to detail. I write clean, well-documented code and catch bugs 
early through thorough testing. This helps maintain code quality and reduces 
technical debt."

### Weakness Questions (B1-B2)
1. What are your weaknesses?
2. What is your biggest weakness?
3. What areas do you need to improve?
4. What do you struggle with?
5. What would your manager say you need to work on?
6. Tell me about a time you failed.

### Sample Weakness Answers (Following "Weakness + Improvement" Formula)

**Example 1: Public speaking**
"I used to be nervous about presenting to large groups. However, I've been 
working on this by volunteering to present in team meetings and taking online 
courses. I'm much more comfortable now, though I still have room to improve."

**Example 2: Impatience**
"Sometimes I can be impatient when things move slowly. I like to work efficiently 
and get frustrated with delays. I'm learning to be more patient and understand 
that some processes take time for good reasons."

**Example 3: Over-committing**
"I sometimes take on too many tasks because I want to help everyone. This can 
affect my productivity. I'm learning to prioritize better and say no when 
necessary to maintain quality work."

**Example 4: Perfectionism**
"I can be a perfectionist, which sometimes means I spend too much time on details. 
I'm working on finding the balance between quality and efficiency, and learning 
when 'good enough' is actually good enough."

**Example 5: Specific technical skill**
"I have limited experience with [specific technology], but I'm actively learning 
it through online courses and personal projects. I'm confident I can master it 
quickly based on my track record of learning new technologies."

## 5. WORK EXPERIENCE & BACKGROUND

### Basic Questions (A2-B1)
1. Tell me about your work experience.
2. What did you do in your last job?
3. Why did you leave your last job?
4. How long did you work at your last company?
5. What was your role in your previous company?

### Detailed Questions (B1-B2)
6. Why are you leaving your current job?
7. What did you like most about your previous job?
8. What did you like least about your previous job?
9. Describe a typical day at your current job.
10. What was your biggest achievement in your last role?
11. What was the most challenging project you worked on?
12. Why have you changed jobs frequently? (if applicable)

### Sample Answers for "Why are you leaving?"

**Good Reasons:**
- "I'm looking for new challenges and opportunities to grow"
- "I want to work with [specific technology] that your company uses"
- "I'm seeking a role with more responsibility"
- "The company is restructuring / my project ended"
- "I want to work for a larger/smaller company"
- "I'm looking for better work-life balance"
- "I want to work on [specific type of products/services]"

**How to Handle Difficult Reasons:**

**If fired:**
"The company and I mutually agreed it wasn't the right fit. I learned a lot from 
that experience about what I'm looking for in a workplace. I've reflected on what 
went wrong and have made changes to improve."

**If conflict with manager:**
"I was looking for a different management style that better supports my working 
approach. I work best in an environment where [describe positive environment]."

**If bad company culture:**
"I'm looking for a company culture that values [positive things you want]. 
I believe your company offers that based on [research you've done]."

## 8. BEHAVIORAL & SITUATIONAL QUESTIONS

### Conflict & Challenges (B2-C1)
1. Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a coworker. How did you handle it?
2. Describe a situation where you disagreed with your manager.
3. Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?
4. How do you handle difficult team members?
5. Give me an example of a challenging situation and how you overcame it.
6. Tell me about a time you had to work with someone you didn't like.

### Achievement & Success (B1-B2)
7. Tell me about your biggest accomplishment.
8. Describe a project you're proud of.
9. Tell me about a time you went above and beyond your job duties.
10. Give me an example of when you showed leadership.

### Learning & Adaptation (B1-B2)
11. Tell me about a time you had to learn something new quickly.
12. Describe a situation where you made a mistake. How did you fix it?
13. Tell me about a time you received criticism. How did you respond?
14. Give me an example of when you had to adapt to a major change.

### STAR Method for Answering (Teach candidates this structure)

**S**ituation: Describe the context
**T**ask: Explain what needed to be done
**A**ction: Detail what you did
**R**esult: Share the outcome

**Example:**
"In my previous job (Situation), we had a critical bug in production that was 
affecting users (Task). I quickly identified the root cause, implemented a fix, 
and coordinated with the team to deploy it (Action). We resolved the issue within 
2 hours and prevented data loss (Result)."

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### ‚ùå Communication Mistakes
1. **Speaking too quietly** - Speak clearly and confidently
2. **Not asking for clarification** - If you don't understand, say: "Could you please repeat that?" or "Could you explain what you mean by...?"
3. **Using memorized answers robotically** - Be natural and genuine
4. **Saying "I don't know" and stopping** - Add: "but I'm willing to learn" or "I can find out"
5. **Interrupting the interviewer** - Wait for them to finish

### ‚ùå Content Mistakes
1. **Badmouthing previous employers** - Stay professional and positive
2. **Being too modest** - Vietnamese culture values modesty, but interviews require self-promotion
3. **Lying about skills or experience** - Be honest; they will verify
4. **Discussing personal problems** - Keep it professional
5. **Talking about salary too early** - Wait for HR to bring it up
6. **Appearing desperate** - Show confidence in your value
7. **Not researching the company** - Always prepare beforehand

### ‚ùå Body Language Mistakes (Even in English interviews)
1. **Avoiding eye contact** - Shows lack of confidence
2. **Fidgeting or poor posture** - Sit up straight, appear engaged
3. **Not smiling** - Be friendly and approachable
4. **Looking at phone** - Give full attention

## USEFUL PHRASES FOR DIFFERENT SITUATIONS

### When You Need Time to Think
- "That's a good question. Let me think for a moment..."
- "Could I have a moment to consider that?"
- "Let me gather my thoughts..."

### When You Don't Understand
- "I'm sorry, could you repeat that?"
- "Could you rephrase the question?"
- "I'm not sure I understood. Do you mean...?"
- "Could you speak a bit more slowly, please?"

### When You Don't Know the Answer
- "I'm not familiar with that specifically, but..."
- "I haven't encountered that situation yet, but I would..."
- "That's not my area of expertise, but I'm willing to learn."

### When You Want to Elaborate
- "To give you more context..."
- "Let me explain further..."
- "For example..."
- "What I mean is..."

### Showing Enthusiasm
- "I'm very excited about this opportunity because..."
- "That sounds really interesting!"
- "I'd love to be part of..."
- "This aligns perfectly with my goals."

### Professional Transitions
- "Building on that..."
- "Related to what you just asked..."
- "That reminds me of..."
- "Another example would be..."

## ENGLISH PROFICIENCY LEVELS NEEDED

### Minimum Level for HR Interview: **B1 (Intermediate)**
- Can understand main points of clear standard speech
- Can handle most common situations
- Can describe experiences, events, and goals
- Can give reasons and explanations

### Comfortable Level: **B2 (Upper-Intermediate)**
- Can interact with fluency and spontaneity
- Can explain viewpoints with advantages and disadvantages
- Can understand detailed discussions

### International Companies: **B2-C1**
- Regular communication with foreign managers
- Client-facing roles
- Documentation in English

## ASSESSMENT RUBRIC FOR HR

### Vocabulary & Grammar (20%)
- Uses appropriate professional vocabulary
- Minimal grammar errors that don't impede understanding
- Can form complex sentences

### Fluency & Pronunciation (20%)
- Speaks at reasonable pace with minimal hesitation
- Pronunciation is clear enough to understand
- Can self-correct naturally

### Comprehension (20%)
- Understands questions without multiple repetitions
- Responds relevantly to questions
- Asks for clarification appropriately

### Content & Relevance (25%)
- Provides relevant, detailed answers
- Uses examples effectively
- Answers demonstrate self-awareness and professionalism

### Professional Communication (15%)
- Maintains professional tone
- Shows appropriate enthusiasm
- Demonstrates confidence without arrogance
- Handles difficult questions tactfully

## RED FLAGS

### Communication Red Flags
- Cannot understand basic questions even after repetition
- Gives irrelevant answers consistently
- Shows extreme nervousness that prevents communication
- Cannot express basic ideas clearly
- Refuses to ask for clarification when clearly confused

### Content Red Flags
- Badmouths previous employers aggressively
- Cannot explain job gaps or frequent job changes
- Inconsistent information about experience
- Unrealistic salary expectations without justification
- Shows no knowledge about the company
- Cannot articulate career goals
- Blames others for all problems
- Shows no weaknesses or areas for improvement

### Attitude Red Flags
- Appears disinterested or unmotivated
- Arrogant or dismissive
- Unprepared (doesn't know own resume)
- Late without explanation
- Inappropriate dress or behavior

---

## GREEN FLAGS

### Communication Green Flags
- Asks for clarification appropriately
- Self-corrects errors naturally
- Uses professional vocabulary
- Shows confidence despite language imperfections
- Adapts communication style based on understanding

### Content Green Flags
- Well-researched about company
- Provides specific examples with details
- Shows self-awareness about strengths and weaknesses
- Has clear career goals aligned with position
- Demonstrates problem-solving approach
- Shows enthusiasm for role
- Asks thoughtful questions

### Attitude Green Flags
- Professional and courteous
- Positive attitude about past experiences
- Takes responsibility for mistakes
- Shows growth mindset
- Punctual and prepared
- Appropriate follow-up after interview

## FINAL TIPS FOR CANDIDATES

1. **Prepare, but don't over-rehearse** - Sound natural, not robotic
2. **Be honest** - Lying will catch up with you
3. **Show enthusiasm** - Companies want motivated employees
4. **Focus on value** - What can you bring to the company?
5. **Ask questions** - Shows interest and engagement
6. **Follow up** - Send a thank you email
7. **Learn from each interview** - Take notes on difficult questions
8. **Practice speaking English regularly** - Not just before interviews
9. **Research salary** - Know your worth and market rates
10. **Be yourself** - Authentic candidates make better matches
